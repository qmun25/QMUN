Messages from the Secretariat

Message from the Secretary-General

— Aarav Dhanuka

Esteemed delegates, distinguished guests, and honorable members of the international community — welcome to the tenth edition of the SelaQui International School Model United Nations Conference. This gathering is a testament to our commitment to dialogue, collaboration, and innovation among young minds.

As we embark on this journey, let us reaffirm our dedication to upholding the values of cooperation and informed debate that have guided QMUN since its inception. This year's theme, 'Diplomacy in the Digital Age,' challenges us to navigate the complexities of modern international relations while maintaining the timeless principles of mutual respect and understanding.

I encourage each delegate to engage thoughtfully, listen actively, and contribute meaningfully to the discussions ahead. Together, we can forge solutions that reflect the aspirations of a connected yet diverse global community.

Message from the President

— Karan Bhirani

Welcome to QMUN'25! This year's theme, 'Diplomacy in the Digital Age,' reflects our commitment to addressing contemporary global challenges through innovative solutions and collaborative dialogue. As President, I am excited to witness the exceptional debates and resolutions that will define this conference.

Our five committees will tackle pressing issues from Syrian conflict resolution to maritime security, from economic sanctions to the evolving role of traditional institutions in modern democracies. I urge all participants to approach these discussions with the gravity they deserve, bringing both passion and pragmatism to the floor.

The future of diplomacy lies in your hands, and I have complete confidence in your ability to rise to the occasion and deliver outcomes that honor the spirit of the United Nations.

United Nations Security Council

Chair: Aarav Dhanuka | Email: dhanuka.aarav@gmail.com

Ensuring Peace and Political Stability in Syria through Conflict De-escalation and Inclusive Transition

About the Committee

​ Dear Delegates, ​ I, Aarav Dhanuka, the Secretary-General of the 2025 SelaQui International School Model United Nations Conference (QMUN) and the Chairperson of the United Nations Security Council, extend a warm welcome to each one of you. ​ As future diplomats and leaders, you hold an essential responsibility in shaping a world rooted in peace, cooperation, and justice. QMUN provides a dynamic platform to sharpen your skills in diplomacy, negotiation, and critical thinking — skills that are more relevant than ever in today’s turbulent world. ​

This year, the United Nations Security Council will convene to deliberate on the agenda: “Ensuring Peace and Political Stability in Syria through Conflict De-escalation and Inclusive Transition.” The crisis in Syria continues to pose one of the gravest threats to global peace and regional stability. Delegates will be called upon to engage in high-level discourse, propose realistic solutions, and strive for consensus in the face of geopolitical complexity. ​

A detailed background guide will be shared to provide a strong foundation for your research, outlining key facts, perspectives, and points of debate. However, your success in committee will depend on going beyond the guide — through independent research, strategic thinking, and unwavering advocacy for your nation’s policies and priorities. ​

I am confident that your dedication, intellect, and diplomacy will make this simulation not only impactful but also unforgettable. ​

Wishing you all the very best — I look forward to seeing the Security Council in action. ​

Warm regards,
Aarav Dhanuka
Secretary-General
QMUN 2025

Disarmament & International Security Committee

Chair: Karan Bhirani | Email: karanbhirani26@gmail.com

Deliberation on the Peaceful Resolution of Territorial Conflicts and the Strengthening of Maritime Security Frameworks

About the Committee

Dear Delegates, ​ I am Karan Bhirani, both the President of the SelaQui International School Model United Nations Conference (QMUN) 2025 and the Chair of the Disarmament and International Security Committee (DISEC). It is my great privilege to warmly welcome you to this conference. ​
​
This year, DISEC will be addressing a critically important agenda: “Deliberation on the Peaceful Resolution of Territorial Conflicts and the Strengthening of Maritime Security Frameworks in Strategically Vital International Trade Corridors.” As global tensions rise over territorial disputes and the security of key maritime trade routes, ensuring the safety and stability of these crucial international waters is more important than ever. As delegates, you will engage in complex discussions on how to resolve territorial conflicts, enhance cooperative frameworks, and ensure that international maritime trade remains secure and uninterrupted. ​​
​
A comprehensive background guide will be provided to assist you in understanding the key issues and areas for discussion. However, the true success of your role will depend on your independent research, diplomatic skill, and ability to collaborate with others to find peaceful and effective solutions to these pressing challenges. I have full confidence in your intellectual capacity, diplomatic finesse, and commitment to peace. Let this conference be a space where creative solutions are born, diverse perspectives are valued, and meaningful progress is made toward a safer, more secure maritime future. ​​
​
I look forward to a challenging, insightful, and productive conference. ​​
​
Best regards,​
Karan Bhirani​
President, QMUN 2025​

United Nations Economic and Social Council

Chair: Sarwagya Raj Gupta

Evaluating the Global Impact of Trade Wars and Economic Sanctions on International Markets

About the Committee

​​​
​ Dear Delegates, ​
​
I, Sarwagya Raj Gupta, the Chair of the United Nations Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC) for the 2025 SelaQui International School Model United Nations Conference (QMUN), extend a warm welcome to you all.
​​
This year’s agenda — “Evaluating the Global Impact of Trade Wars and Economic Sanctions on International Markets” — is one of great contemporary relevance. In a world increasingly shaped by shifting alliances, protectionist policies, and geopolitical tensions, the ripple effects of economic confrontations are being felt across borders. From supply chain disruptions to inflationary pressures and market instability, the implications are far-reaching and complex. ​
​
As delegates, you are tasked with dissecting these challenges and proposing balanced, innovative solutions. ECOSOC is a platform that requires not only an understanding of international economics and trade dynamics but also empathy for the human cost of these policies. ​​
​
A background guide will provide you with a foundation, but it is your independent research, critical thinking, and diplomatic skill that will truly drive the debate forward. We encourage you to analyze data, understand national perspectives, and propose sustainable frameworks for global cooperation. ​​
​
I look forward to watching your ideas unfold and your diplomacy in action. May this committee be filled with thoughtful engagement and transformative dialogue. ​​
​
See you in committee! ​
​
Best regards,​
Sarwagya Raj Gupta​
Chair, ECOSOC – QMUN 2025

All India Political Parties Meet

Chair: Gul Chaudhary

Assessing the Relevance and Functioning of the Waqf Act in a Secular Democracy

About the Committee

​ To the Delegates, I, Gul Chaudhary, the Chair of the All India Political Parties Meet (AIPPM), extend a warm and cordial welcome to the ninth edition of QMUN, 2025. We are delighted to have you as part of this intellectually engaging committee and look forward to two days of rich discourse and impactful resolutions. ​
​
The agenda before us — “Assessing the Relevance and Functioning of the Waqf Act in a Secular Democracy” — is one that strikes at the heart of India’s constitutional ethos. It raises important questions on the balance between religious administration and secular governance, and it demands from you a nuanced, well-reasoned approach rooted in political awareness and legal understanding. ​​
​
To help guide your research, we have provided a background guide. However, we strongly encourage you to go beyond its scope — to dig deeper, analyze diverse perspectives, and come prepared with arguments that reflect both your portfolio's ideology and the broader national interest. ​​
​
Each portfolio has been assigned with careful thought, ensuring that every delegate plays a meaningful role in shaping the direction of this conversation. The AIPPM is not a mere debate — it is a political battlefield where diplomacy, rhetoric, and logic converge. ​​
​
We trust that your participation will reflect passion, preparation, and a commitment to constructive dialogue. ​
​
Wishing you all the best! ​
​
Gul Chaudhary​
​ Chair, AIPPM

International Press Corps

Chair: Aarav Mittal

Press Coverage and Creative Journalism

About the Committee

​ Dear Delegates, ​
​
I am honoured to welcome you to the QMUN'25 International Press Corps (IPC). My name is Aarav Mittal, and I have the privilege of serving as your IPC Chairperson. ​​
​
Unlike other committees where debate and negotiation take the lead, the IPC committee at QMUN is driven by the power of words and the art of reporting. You will step into the role of a journalist — using your writing to communicate, inform, and influence. This is where the famous adage, “The pen is mightier than the sword,” holds true. ​​
​
Here in IPC, we recognize the immense responsibility of the press to provide unbiased, objective, and impactful coverage. The media holds the unique ability to shape public opinion, define narratives, and hold power to account. Therefore, as delegates of IPC, your ability to adopt neutrality, objectivity, and transparency is vital in achieving success. ​​
​
To enhance the experience at QMUN, we are excited to feature ‘Originals’ in the press coverage. Delegates are invited to submit creative content — such as a short documentary capturing key moments, caricatures illustrating significant events, or any form of creative writing like poems, sonnets, or comics. Selected ‘Originals’ will be featured in the QMUN newsletter. ​
​
Your organisation, creativity, and precision in reporting will be the foundation of your success in IPC. I am confident that your skills will contribute to a vibrant and effective press presence throughout the conference. ​​
​
Wishing everyone the best of luck! ​
​
Aarav Mittal​
Chair, International Press Corps – QMUN 2025
